Helping injured British wildlife recover

Jacob Wildlife Centre is a small rescue and rehabilitation centre near Liverpool. We care for sick, injured and orphaned wild animals and return them to the wild whenever it is safe to do so.

Meet the animals See centre map

Why this centre exists

Every year, many British animals are hurt by cars, litter, garden tools or loss of habitat. Without help, many of them would not survive. Jacob Wildlife Centre provides emergency care, safe enclosures and professional treatment so that wildlife can return to healthy, natural lives.

When release is not possible, the centre offers a calm, enriched environment for animals that need a permanent home.

Our work in numbers

120+ animals treated each year
65% released back into the wild
40 local schools we work with

How we help

Rescue

Local residents, vets and organisations contact the centre when they find injured or orphaned wildlife. Our volunteers help to transport animals safely to the centre.

Rehabilitate

Animals receive medical treatment, food, clean water and quiet spaces. Staff carefully monitor their health and behaviour every day.

Release or lifelong care

When animals are strong enough, they are released back into suitable habitats. Others stay at the centre in long-term enclosures designed for their needs.
